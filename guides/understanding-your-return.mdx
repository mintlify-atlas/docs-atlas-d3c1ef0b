After EZFile processes your W-2 and completes your tax calculations, you'll receive a detailed return summary. This guide explains each section and what the numbers mean.

## Return Summary Format

EZFile generates two files in the `returns/` directory:

  <Card title="summary-2025.md" icon="file-lines">

## Reading the Summary

### Income Section

```markdown
Wages (Line 1a):              $52,000.00
Total Income (Line 9):        $52,000.00
```

- **Line 1a**: Total wages from all W-2s (Box 1)
- **Line 9**: Total income (equals Line 1a for W-2-only filers)

### Adjustments Section

```markdown
Student Loan Interest (Schedule 1, Line 21):  $1,200.00
Total Adjustments (Line 10):                  $1,200.00
Adjusted Gross Income (Line 11):             $50,800.00
```

- **Schedule 1, Line 21**: Student loan interest deduction (max $2,500, subject to phaseout)
- **Line 10**: Total adjustments to income
- **Line 11**: AGI = Total Income - Adjustments (this is your most important number)

<Note>
Your AGI determines eligibility for many credits and deductions. Lower AGI = more tax benefits.
</Note>

### Deductions Section

```markdown
Standard Deduction (Line 12a):    $15,750.00
Schedule 1-A Deductions (Line 12c):   $500.00
Total Deductions (Line 14):        $16,250.00
Taxable Income (Line 15):          $34,550.00
```

- **Line 12a**: Standard deduction ($15,750 under 65, $17,750 if 65+)
- **Line 12c**: Schedule 1-A deductions (tips, overtime, car loan interest, senior deduction)
- **Line 14**: Total deductions
- **Line 15**: Taxable income = AGI - Total Deductions (this is what gets taxed)

### Tax Calculation Section

```markdown
Tax Brackets (Single, 2025):
  10% on $11,925:               $1,192.50
  12% on $22,625:               $2,715.00
Total Tax (Line 16):             $3,907.50
```

EZFile shows exactly how your tax is calculated using the 2025 single filer brackets:

| Income Range | Rate | Your Income in Bracket | Tax |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0 - $11,925 | 10% | $11,925 | $1,192.50 |
| $11,926 - $34,550 | 12% | $22,625 | $2,715.00 |
| **Total** | | | **$3,907.50** |

<Warning>
Taxable income below $0 is treated as $0. You never get a "negative" tax.
</Warning>

### Credits Section

```markdown
Nonrefundable Credits:
  Saver's Credit (Form 8880):      $200.00
Total Nonrefundable Credits:       $200.00
Tax After Credits (Line 22):     $3,707.50
```

- **Nonrefundable credits** reduce your tax but can't make it negative
- **Refundable credits** (like EIC) can give you money even if you owe no tax

### Payments and Refund Section

```markdown
Federal Tax Withheld (Line 25a):   $8,400.00
Earned Income Credit (Line 27a):       $0.00
Total Payments (Line 33):          $8,400.00

Total Tax (Line 24):               $3,707.50
YOUR REFUND (Line 34):             $4,692.50
```

- **Line 25a**: Federal tax withheld from your W-2(s) (Box 2)
- **Line 27a**: Earned Income Credit (refundable)
- **Line 33**: Total payments and refundable credits
- **Line 34**: Refund = Total Payments - Total Tax (if positive)
- **Line 37**: Amount Owed = Total Tax - Total Payments (if positive)

<Note>
You'll either have a refund OR amount owed, never both.
</Note>

## State and Local Tax Summary

If your W-2 includes state/local taxes, EZFile shows:

```markdown
STATE: Pennsylvania
State Wages (Box 16):          $47,808.35
State Rate:                    3.07%
State Tax:                     $1,467.72
State Withheld (Box 17):       $1,467.72
State Refund/Owed:                 $0.00

LOCAL: Pittsburgh (PSD 500101)
Local Wages (Box 18):          $47,808.35
Local Rate:                    3.0%
Local Tax:                     $1,434.25
Local Withheld (Box 19):       $1,434.25
Local Refund/Owed:                 $0.00
```

### Key Differences

- **State wages** (Box 16) may differ from federal wages (Box 1)
- Some states (like PA) tax retirement contributions that the federal government doesn't
- **Local taxes** are common in PA, OH, and a few other states

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why is my state refund different from federal?">
    State and federal calculations are independent:
    - Different tax rates and brackets
    - Different deductions (many states don't allow student loan deduction)
    - Different wage bases (Box 16 vs Box 1)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if my taxable income is negative?">
    If AGI minus deductions is negative, taxable income becomes $0. You don't get a "negative tax," but your tax will be $0 before credits.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why do I see cents in some places but not others?">
    EZFile calculates with exact cents throughout but rounds only at final steps per IRS rules. Form 1040 lines show whole dollars.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I owe instead of getting a refund?">
    If Total Tax > Total Payments, you'll see "Amount Owed" instead of "Refund." You can pay when filing or set up a payment plan with the IRS.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
