## Try EZFile with Sample Data

EZFile includes a sample W-2 with fictional data so you can test the plugin without using real tax documents. You'll see exactly how EZFile processes a W-2 and calculates a complete federal return.

<Note>
  The sample W-2 contains completely fictional data — no real personal information.
</Note>

## Process the Sample W-2

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run the file-taxes command">
    If you installed from the repository:
    ```bash
    /ezfile:file-taxes ~/ezfile/Sample-W2.pdf
    ```
    
    If you installed from the marketplace, first download the sample:
    ```bash
    curl -o ~/Sample-W2.pdf https://raw.githubusercontent.com/stat-guy/ezFile/main/Sample-W2.pdf
    /ezfile:file-taxes ~/Sample-W2.pdf
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the W-2 extraction">
    EZFile will read the W-2 and display the extracted information:
    
    - Employer name (University of Pittsburgh)
    - Wages from Box 1
    - Federal tax withheld from Box 2
    - Social Security Number (masked to last 4 digits)
    - State and local information
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Answer intake questions">
    EZFile will ask a few questions to complete your return:
    
    - Date of birth
    - Student loan interest (if any)
    - Tips at work
    - Overtime pay
    - New car purchase in 2025
    
    For the sample W-2, you can provide realistic fictional answers to see how they affect the calculation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View your calculated return">
    EZFile calculates your complete Form 1040 line by line:
    
    ```
    FEDERAL RETURN SUMMARY
    Wages:                    $XX,XXX.XX
    Student loan interest:    -$X,XXX.XX
    AGI:                      $XX,XXX.XX
    Standard deduction:      -$15,750.00
    Taxable income:           $XX,XXX.XX

    Tax:
      10% on $X,XXX:          $X,XXX.XX
      12% on $X,XXX:          $X,XXX.XX
    Total tax:                $X,XXX.XX

    Federal withheld:         $X,XXX.XX
    YOUR REFUND:              $X,XXX.XX
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Every number in your return traces back to either a W-2 box or an IRS constant. EZFile shows its math at every step.
</Tip>

## Sample Return Output

The sample W-2 (for fictional employee Elizabeth A Darling at University of Pittsburgh) produces a **$4,404.60 federal refund**.

## Understanding Your Return

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is AGI?">
    Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) is your total income minus certain deductions like student loan interest. It's used to calculate eligibility for many tax benefits.
    
    Try: `/ezfile:explain AGI`
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How are tax brackets applied?">
    Tax brackets are marginal — you pay 10% on the first $11,925, then 12% on the amount above that up to $48,475, and so on. EZFile shows the exact calculation for each bracket.
    
    Try: `/ezfile:explain tax brackets`
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What is the standard deduction?">
    For 2025, single filers under 65 get a $15,750 standard deduction. This amount is subtracted from your AGI before calculating tax. If you're 65 or older, you get $17,750.
    
    Try: `/ezfile:explain standard deduction`
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What is Schedule 1-A?">
    New for 2025, Schedule 1-A provides above-the-line deductions for tips, overtime pay, car loan interest, and seniors. These reduce your AGI directly.
    
    Try: `/ezfile:explain schedule 1-a`
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
